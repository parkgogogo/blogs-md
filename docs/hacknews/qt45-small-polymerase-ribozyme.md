# Qt45: A small polymerase ribozyme that can synthesize itself

How life arose from simple chemical building blocks remains one of science's greatest unanswered questions. Early life would have required a way to store and transmit genetic information without the complex molecular machinery used by modern organisms. RNA has long been proposed as a central player in this process because it can act both as a catalyst and as an instructive template for replication. This dual function of RNA potentially enables both heredity and replication within a single molecule, a prerequisite for the evolution of more complex forms.

According to the "RNA World" hypothesis, such RNA molecules may have formed spontaneously in a primordial soup, eventually acquiring the ability to self-replicate and evolve. However, most known catalytic RNAs, or ribozymes, discovered so far are too large and too complex to either replicate themselves or to plausibly arise spontaneously.

A recent study from Philipp Holliger's group in the LMB's PNAC Division addresses this problem by identifying a new small polymerase ribozyme that may overcome these fundamental limitations.

RNA self-replication is defined as the ability of an RNA molecule to copy both itself and the information encoded in its complementary strand, i.e. its template. Over the past 25 years, ribozymes that can copy RNA (polymerase ribozymes) have been identified but they are generally large and structurally complex, making self-replication reactions difficult.

Because no shorter ribozymes with this activity have been found, it has been widely assumed that it is only large and complex RNAs that can catalyse RNA copying reactions. This assumption leads to a paradox regarding the origin of the first self-replicating RNA: it would need to be large and complex enough to catalyse its own replication, yet that very size and complexity hinder self-replication and make its spontaneous origin highly unlikely.

As such, if this catalytic activity can be found in smaller, simpler RNA molecules, this paradox could be resolved, with important implications for the earliest steps in the origin of life.

Led by Investigator Scientist Edoardo Gianni, the group applied in vitro selection to renew the search for active ribozymes in pools of short, random RNA sequences. The process was repeated several times to enrich the active sequences. In the end, they identified three small, unrelated RNA sequences that exhibited polymerase ribozyme activity.

These RNAs underwent further laboratory-based evolution to improve their catalytic activity, resulting in an unexpectedly small ribozyme of 45 nucleotides with robust RNA polymerase activity, named "Quite Tiny 45" (QT45).

QT45 is a 45-nucleotide polymerase ribozyme, discovered from random sequence pools, that catalyzes general RNA-templated RNA synthesis using trinucleotide triphosphate (triplet) substrates in mildly alkaline eutectic ice.

To further characterise QT45, the group evaluated its capacity to copy a range of RNA sequences distinct from its own, thereby assessing its generality across multiple RNA templates. QT45 demonstrated the ability to copy RNA templates of increased length and structural complexity. Notably, it successfully copied another ribozyme known as the Hammerhead ribozyme. To date, such a complex synthesis has been reported only for large and complex ribozymes.

Furthermore, QT45 was shown to be able to use a range of different RNA substrates for its copying reaction. This observed promiscuity in the substrates and RNA templates copied by QT45 indicates that RNA replication can likely occur within a chemically diverse prebiotic environment.

Once the generality of QT45 was established, the group investigated its ability to synthesise both itself and its complementary strand. The synthesis of itself uses the ribozyme's complementary strand as the template, whereby the synthesis of the complementary strand uses the ribozyme itself as the template – exploiting the dual function of RNA acting as both a template strand and as a catalyst.

The experiments confirmed that QT45 could carry out both syntheses in two separate reactions, individually completing the two steps of a self-replication cycle.

QT45 can synthesize both its complementary strand using a random triplet pool at 94.1% per-nucleotide fidelity, and a copy of itself using defined substrates, both with yields of ~0.2% in 72 days. The discovery of polymerase activity in a small RNA motif suggests that polymerase ribozymes are likely much more abundant than previously anticipated.

Ultimately, identification of this small ribozyme has shown that catalytic RNAs with polymerase function can be much simpler and are likely much more abundant than previously anticipated. This lends support to the spontaneous emergence and self-replication of RNA at the dawn of life.

# 中文翻译

Qt45：一种能够自我合成的小型聚合酶核酶

生命如何从简单的化学构建块中产生，仍然是科学界最重大的未解之谜之一。早期生命需要一种在不使用现代生物体复杂分子机器的情况下存储和传递遗传信息的方式。RNA长期以来被认为是这一过程中的核心角色，因为它既可以作为催化剂，又可以作为复制的指导模板。RNA的这种双重功能可能使遗传和复制在单个分子中实现，这是进化出更复杂形式的先决条件。

根据"RNA世界"假说，这样的RNA分子可能在原始汤中自发形成，最终获得自我复制和进化的能力。然而，迄今为止发现的大多数已知催化RNA（即核酶）都太大且太复杂，既无法自我复制，也不太可能自发产生。

最近，来自LMB（分子生物学实验室）PNAC部门Philipp Holliger团队的一项研究通过识别一种新的小型聚合酶核酶，解决了这一难题，这种核酶可能克服了这些根本性限制。

RNA自我复制被定义为RNA分子复制自身以及复制其互补链（即模板）中编码信息的能力。在过去的25年里，已经发现了能够复制RNA的核酶（聚合酶核酶），但它们通常体积庞大且结构复杂，使得自我复制反应变得困难。

由于此前未发现具有这种活性的更短核酶，人们普遍认为只有大型且复杂的RNA才能催化RNA复制反应。这种假设导致了关于第一个自我复制RNA起源的悖论：它需要足够大且复杂以催化自身复制，但正是这种大小和复杂性阻碍了自我复制，并使其自发起源极不可能发生。

因此，如果能在更小、更简单的RNA分子中发现这种催化活性，这个悖论就可能得到解决，这对生命起源的最初步骤具有重要意义。

在研究员Edoardo Gianni的领导下，该团队应用体外选择技术，重新在短随机RNA序列池中搜索活性核酶。该过程重复多次以富集活性序列。最终，他们识别出三个小的、不相关的表现出聚合酶核酶活性的RNA序列。

这些RNA经过进一步的实验室进化以提高其催化活性，最终产生了一个出乎意料的小核酶——仅由45个核苷酸组成，具有强大的RNA聚合酶活性，被命名为"Quite Tiny 45"（QT45，意为"相当小的45"）。

QT45是一个由45个核苷酸组成的聚合酶核酶，从随机序列库中发现，能够在弱碱性共晶冰中使用三核苷酸磷酸（三联体）底物催化一般的RNA模板化RNA合成。

为了进一步表征QT45，该团队评估了其复制一系列与其自身不同的RNA序列的能力，从而评估其在多种RNA模板上的通用性。QT45展示了复制长度和结构复杂性增加的RNA模板的能力。值得注意的是，它成功复制了另一种被称为"锤头核酶"（Hammerhead ribozyme）的核酶。迄今为止，这种复杂的合成仅对大型复杂核酶有报道。

此外，QT45被证明能够使用一系列不同的RNA底物进行复制反应。QT45在底物和RNA模板复制方面表现出的这种混杂性表明，RNA复制很可能可以在化学多样性的前生物环境中发生。

在确定了QT45的通用性后，该团队研究了其合成自身及其互补链的能力。合成自身时使用核酶的互补链作为模板，而合成互补链则使用核酶本身作为模板——利用RNA作为模板链和催化剂的双重功能。

实验证实，QT45可以在两个独立的反应中完成这两种合成，分别完成自我复制循环的两个步骤。

QT45能够使用随机三联体池以其互补链为模板进行合成，每个核苷酸的保真度达到94.1%，并且可以使用确定的底物以自身为模板进行复制，两种情况下72天的产率均约为0.2%。在小型RNA基序中发现聚合酶活性表明，聚合酶核酶可能比之前预期的更为丰富。

最终，这种小型核酶的识别表明，具有聚合酶功能的催化RNA可以比先前预期的更简单，而且可能更为丰富。这为生命起源初期RNA的自发出现和自我复制提供了支持。
