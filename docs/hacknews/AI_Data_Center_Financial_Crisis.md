The key difference here is that unlike operating leases, finance leases are effectively long-term loans where the borrower is expected to retain ownership of the asset — whether that's GPUs or a building — at the end of the contract. Traditionally, such arrangements have been used to finance relatively limited-lifespan parts of data centers — like computer hardware, which becomes obsolete over time.

这里的关键区别在于，与经营租赁不同，融资租赁实际上是长期贷款，借款人被期望在合同结束时保留资产（无论是GPU还是建筑物）的所有权。传统上，这类安排被用于为数据中心中使用寿命相对有限的部件提供融资——比如计算机硬件，这些硬件会随着时间的推移而过时。

## Training Costs Should Count Towards AI Labs' Gross Margins, and Excluding Them Is Deceptive / 训练成本应该计入AI实验室的毛利率，不包括它们是欺骗性的

However, the most deceptive part is likely this word "training." When you read "training," you're meant to think "oh, this is preparation for something, this is an R&D cost," and "training large language models" is actually a persistent cost similar to inference (generating an output) or any other kind of maintenance.

然而，最具欺骗性的部分可能是这个"训练"一词。当你读到"训练"时，你应该想到"哦，这是为某事做的准备，这是研发成本"，而"训练大语言模型"实际上是一种与推理（生成输出）或任何其他维护一样持续的成本。

While most people know about pretraining — feeding large amounts of data into a model (I know this is a simplification) — in practice, large amounts of current models use [post-training](https://towardsdatascience.com/different-ways-of-training-llms-c57885f388ed/?ref=wheresyoured.at), covering everything from small adjustments to model behavior to full reinforcement learning where experts reward or punish responses to particular prompts.

虽然大多数人都知道预训练——将大量数据输入模型（我知道这是一种简化）——但实际上，当前大量模型使用[后训练](https://towardsdatascience.com/different-ways-of-training-llms-c57885f388ed/?ref=wheresyoured.at)，涵盖从模型行为的微小调整到全面的强化学习，专家会对特定提示的响应进行奖励或惩罚。

To be clear, all of this is well-known and well-documented, but the term "training" implies that it might one day stop, and the truth is: training costs are increasing dramatically, "training" covers anything from training new models to bug-fixes on existing ones, and, more fundamentally, it's a persistent cost — an essential and unavoidable cost of doing business.

需要明确的是，所有这些都是众所周知且有据可查的，但"训练"这个术语暗示它可能有一天会停止，而真相是：训练成本正在急剧增加，"训练"涵盖从训练新模型到对现有模型进行错误修复的任何事情。而且，更根本的是，这是一项持续的成本——是开展业务必不可少且不可避免的成本。

For AI labs like OpenAI and Anthropic, training is as common (and necessary) a cost as inference (generating an output), [yet it is entirely excluded from gross margins](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-lowers-profit-margin-projection-revenue-skyrockets?rc=kz8jh3&ref=wheresyoured.at):

对于像OpenAI和Anthropic这样的AI实验室来说，训练是一种与生成输出相关的成本（推理）一样常见（且必要）的成本，[然而它被完全排除在毛利率之外](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-lowers-profit-margin-projection-revenue-skyrockets?rc=kz8jh3&ref=wheresyoured.at)：

> Anthropic previously expected gross margins to exceed 70% by 2027, while OpenAI projected gross margins of at least 70% by 2029, which would bring them closer to the gross margins of publicly-listed software and cloud-computing companies. But both AI developers have also spent heavily to rent servers to develop new models — training costs that don't count toward gross margins — making it harder for them to turn a net profit than traditional software companies.

> Anthropic此前预计到2027年毛利率将超过70%，OpenAI则预计到2029年毛利率至少达到70%，这将使它们更接近上市软件和云计算公司的毛利率水平。但这两家AI开发商在租用服务器开发新模型——即训练成本，不计入毛利率——上也花费了大量资金，这使得它们比传统软件公司更难实现净利润。

This is fundamentally deceptive. While one might argue that R&D doesn't count towards gross margins, training does not belong there — yet gross margins usually include the raw materials required to manufacture something, and training is absolutely part of the raw material cost of running an AI model. Direct labor and component parts are treated as part of gross margin calculations, and training spend — including data and the training process itself — is absolutely meaningful, and excluding it is an act of deception.

这本质上是欺骗性的。虽然有人会争辩说研发不计入毛利率，但训练不属于毛利率——然而毛利率通常包括制造某物所需的原材料，而训练绝对是运行AI模型的原材料成本的一部分。直接劳动力和零部件被视为毛利率计算的一部分，而训练支出——包括数据和训练过程本身——绝对是有意义的，将其排除在外是一种欺骗行为。

Anthropic's gross margin for 2025 was 40% — 38% if you include free users of Claude — with inference costs of $2.7 (or $2.79) billion and [training costs of roughly $4.1 billion](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-lowers-profit-margin-projection-revenue-skyrockets?rc=kz8jh3&ref=wheresyoured.at#:~:text=The%20inference%20costs,models%20last%20year.). What happens if we include training costs in that calculation?

Anthropic 2025年的毛利率为40%——如果包括Claude的免费用户则为38%——推理成本为27亿（或27.9亿）美元，[训练成本约为41亿美元](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-lowers-profit-margin-projection-revenue-skyrockets?rc=kz8jh3&ref=wheresyoured.at#:~:text=The%20inference%20costs,models%20last%20year.)。如果把训练成本加入计算会发生什么？

Let's do the math!

让我们来算一算！

- If Anthropic's gross margin in 2025 was 38%, that means its cost of goods sold (COGS) was $2.79 billion.
- If we add training costs, this brings COGS to $6.89 billion, leaving us with negative $2.39 billion after $4.5 billion in revenue.
- This results in a gross margin of negative 53%.

- 如果Anthropic 2025年的毛利率是38%，这意味着其销售成本（COGS）为27.9亿美元。
- 如果我们加上训练成本，这将使销售成本达到68.9亿美元，在45亿美元收入之后，我们剩下-23.9亿美元。
- 这导致毛利率为负53%。

Training is not an upfront cost, and treating it as one is simply to help Anthropic mask its poor business model. Anthropic (like OpenAI) can never stop training, and pretending otherwise is misleading. This isn't just the cost of "training new models," but the cost of maintaining current models, building new products around them, and many other direct, unavoidable components of cost of goods sold. They are manufacturing costs, pure and simple.

训练不是预付成本，将其视为预付成本只是为了帮助Anthropic掩盖其糟糕的商业模型。Anthropic（像OpenAI一样）永远无法停止训练，假装否则是误导性的。这不仅仅是"训练新模型"的成本，而是维护当前模型、围绕它们构建新产品以及许多其他直接、不可避免的销售成本组成部分的成本。它们就是制造成本，简单明了。

Anthropic expects to spend $100 billion on training over the next three years, which means — proportionally to its current costs — it will spend roughly $32 billion on inference over the same period, in addition to the $21 billion TPU purchase, $30 billion Azure spend (which I assume is over the same period?), and the "billions of dollars" on Google Cloud. How does Anthropic pay the bills? Why do outlet after outlet run these fantasy numbers without doing the "how does Anthropic actually get all this money?" math?

Because even by their ridiculous revenue projections, this company is burning money, and things get genuinely worrying when you actually start doing the math on anything related to AI.

这也取决于甲骨文筹集比目前更多的债务——即使在AI炒作周期席卷该公司之前，这已经是一大笔钱了。

[As I discussed a few weeks ago in The Hater's Guide To Oracle](https://www.wheresyoured.at/haters-guide-oracle/#oracle-and-its-partners-have-not-raised-enough-capital-to-pay-for-its-data-centers-%E2%80%94-costs-will-be-at-least-189-billion-in-total-for-45gw-of-data-centers-at-around-42-million-a-megawatt), per Jerome Darling at TD Cowen, data center construction costs are typically around $12-14 million per megawatt of data center IT load (and likely higher due to skilled worker shortages, supply constraints and equipment price increases), with GPU and related hardware costs at $30 million per megawatt. Put simply, Oracle (and its related partners) needs roughly $189 billion to build out the 4.5 gigawatts of Stargate capacity required to make revenue on the OpenAI deal, meaning after raising [$50 billion in debt, bonds and secondary share offerings](https://archive.is/x5jmZ?ref=wheresyoured.at), it still needs roughly $100 billion.

[正如我几周前在《甲骨文黑粉指南》中讨论的](https://www.wheresyoured.at/haters-guide-oracle/#oracle-and-its-partners-have-not-raised-enough-capital-to-pay-for-its-data-centers-%E2%80%94-costs-will-be-at-least-189-billion-in-total-for-45gw-of-data-centers-at-around-42-million-a-megawatt)，根据TD Cowen的Jerome Darling的说法，每兆瓦数据中心IT负载的建设成本通常约为1200-1400万美元（由于技术工人短缺、供应限制和设备价格上涨，实际可能更高），GPU和相关硬件成本为每兆瓦3000万美元。简单来说，甲骨文（及其相关合作伙伴）需要约1890亿美元来建设4.5吉瓦的Stargate容量，以实现OpenAI协议的收入，这意味着在筹集[500亿美元的债务、债券和增发新股](https://archive.is/x5jmZ?ref=wheresyoured.at)之后，它还需要约1000亿美元。

I admit that writing all of this out makes me feel somewhat insane, because doing some very basic math and saying "hey, Oracle doesn't have the capacity and OpenAI doesn't have the money" is somehow a fringe belief. The truth is that it seems like nobody actually wants to talk about the costs of AI, because it's so much easier to say "I'm not a numbers person" or "they'll figure it out."

我承认写出所有这些让我感觉有点疯狂，因为做一些非常基础的数学运算并说"嘿，甲骨文没有容量，OpenAI没有钱"不知何故成了一种边缘信念。事实上，似乎没有人真正想谈论AI的成本，因为说"我不是数字型的人"或"他们会解决的"要容易得多。

This is why in today's newsletter, I will lay out the grim reality of the AI bubble and debut a model I've created to measure the actual real-world costs of AI data centers.

这就是为什么在今天的通讯中，我将阐述AI泡沫的严峻现实，并首次推出我创建的一个模型来衡量AI数据中心的实际真实成本。

While my methodology is complex, my conclusion is simple: Even removing the debt required to build these data centers, operating an AI data center is a mediocre business vulnerable to any change in circumstances.

虽然我的方法很复杂，但我的结论很简单：即使去除建立这些数据中心所需的债务，运营AI数据中心也是一项平庸的业务，容易受到任何情况变化的影响。

Based on hours of discussion with data center professionals, analysts and economists, I've calculated that in most scenarios, the average AI data center operates at gross margins between 30% and 40% — margins that rapidly decline with every day, week or month that a data center isn't operational.

基于与数据中心专业人士、分析师和经济学家的数小时讨论，我计算出，在大多数情况下，平均AI数据中心的毛利率在30%到40%之间——每延迟一天、一周或一个月将数据中心投入运营，这些利润率就会迅速下降。

[This is why Oracle is at negative 100% margins on Nvidia GB200 chips](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/oracle-assures-investors-ai-cloud-margins-struggles-profit-older-nvidia-chips?ref=wheresyoured.at&rc=kz8jh3) — because the heavy upfront costs of building an AI data center (GPUs, servers and other associated equipment) put you billions of dollars in debt before you start providing compute, after which you have to contend with taxes, depreciation, financing and the actual cost of powering the hardware.

[这就是甲骨文在英伟达GB200芯片上出现负100%利润率的原因](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/oracle-assures-investors-ai-cloud-margins-struggles-profit-older-nvidia-chips?ref=wheresyoured.at&rc=kz8jh3)——因为建设AI数据中心的繁重前期成本（GPU、服务器和其他相关设备）让你在开始提供计算服务之前就背负了数十亿美元的债务，之后你还要应对税收、折旧、融资和实际为硬件供电的成本。

Yet things get worse when you factor in the actual financial reality of these deals — and the debt attached to them.

然而，当你将债务成本考虑在内时，情况只会变得更糟。

Based on my current model of the 1 gigawatt Stargate Abilene data center, Oracle likely plans to make roughly $11 billion a year in revenue from 1.2 gigawatts (or roughly 880 megawatts of critical IT load). While that sounds good, when you add depreciation, power costs, $1 billion a year in hosting costs from Crusoe, operating expenses and numerous other costs, its margins are a miserable 27.2% — and that's assuming OpenAI actually pays on time and reliably.

基于我目前对1吉瓦Stargate阿比林（Abilene）数据中心的模型，甲骨文可能计划从1.2吉瓦（或约880兆瓦关键IT负载）中每年获得约110亿美元的收入。虽然这听起来不错，但当你加上折旧、电费、每年来自Crusoe的10亿美元托管成本、运营支出和其他众多成本时，其利润率只有糟糕的27.2%——而且这还是假设OpenAI确实能按时、可靠地付款。

Things only get worse when you factor in the cost of debt. While Oracle is using a combination of bonds and existing cash flow to fund Abilene, it has clearly not received most of the $25 billion+ in GPUs and associated hardware (only 96,000 GPUs have been "delivered"), meaning it likely purchased them with [last September's $18 billion bond issuance](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/oracle-takes-on-18bn-in-debt-ahead-of-ai-data-center-build-out/?ref=wheresyoured.at).

当你将债务成本考虑在内时，情况只会变得更糟。虽然甲骨文使用债券和现有现金流的组合为阿比林提供资金，但它显然尚未收到250亿美元+的GPU和相关硬件的大部分（只有96,000个GPU被"交付"），这意味着它可能是用[去年9月的180亿美元债券发行](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/oracle-takes-on-18bn-in-debt-ahead-of-ai-data-center-build-out/?ref=wheresyoured.at)购买的。

If we assume these calculations, this means Oracle is paying a little under $963 million a year ([per the terms of the bond issuance](https://d18rn0p25nwr6d.cloudfront.net/CIK-0001341439/75771445-e1ef-4297-b255-2b5c92a4f3d2.pdf?ref=wheresyoured.at)), even without a single GPU being turned on, which leaves us with a net profit margin of 22.19%...and that's assuming OpenAI pays every single bill, every single time, with absolutely no delays.

如果我们假设这些计算，这意味着甲骨文每年要支付不到9.63亿美元（[根据债券发行条款](https://d18rn0p25nwr6d.cloudfront.net/CIK-0001341439/75771445-e1ef-4297-b255-2b5c92a4f3d2.pdf?ref=wheresyoured.at)），即使一个GPU都没有开启，这给我们的净利润率留下22.19%……而且这还是假设OpenAI每次都支付每一张账单，而且绝对没有延迟。

These delays are also very, very expensive. Based on my model, if we assume 100 megawatts of critical IT load is running (roughly two buildings and 100,000 GB200s) but has yet to begin generating revenue, Oracle is burning roughly $4.69 million in cash a day. I've also confirmed with sources in Abilene that Stargate Abilene being fully operational in 2026 is impossible.

这些延迟也非常、非常昂贵。基于我的模型，如果我们假设100兆瓦的关键IT负载正在运行（大约两栋建筑和100,000个GB200）但尚未开始产生收入，甲骨文每天大约烧掉469万美元的现金。我还与阿比林的消息来源确认，Stargate阿比林在2026年全面投入运营是不可能的。

To put it simply:

简单来说：

- AI startups are all losing money, and seemingly have no path to sustainability.
- AI data centers are being built in anticipation of demand that does not exist, and will only exist if AI startups — all of which are losing money — can afford to pay for it.
- Oracle has committed to building 4.5 gigawatts of data centers, and burns money every day that OpenAI delays setting up its GPUs, and when it does start making money, it's doing so from a position of having billions of dollars of debt.
- Margins are thin across the entire AI data center operator stack — from landlords like Applied Digital to compute providers like CoreWeave — due to the tens of billions of dollars of debt required to finance both construction and IT hardware, leaving both sides in a position where they're dependent on revenue from hyperscalers or AI startups to fill the gap.
- In a very real sense, the AI compute industry is dependent on AI "succeeding," because if it doesn't, every single one of these data centers becomes a burning hole in the ground.

- AI初创公司都在亏损，而且似乎没有可持续发展的路径。
- AI数据中心是在预期需求的情况下建设的，而这种需求并不存在，只有当AI初创公司——它们都在亏损——能够负担得起支付时，这种需求才会存在。
- 甲骨文已承诺建设4.5吉瓦的数据中心，OpenAI每延迟一天设置其GPU，它就在烧钱，当它开始赚钱时，它是从背负数十亿美元债务的起点开始的。
- 由于资助建设和IT硬件所需的数十亿美元债务，整个AI数据中心运营商堆栈的利润率都很低——从像Applied Digital这样的房东到像CoreWeave这样的计算提供商——这让双方都陷入只能靠来自超大规模云计算公司或AI初创公司的收入来填补的困境。
- 在非常真实的意义上，AI计算行业依赖于AI"成功"，因为如果不成功，每一个这样的数据中心都将成为地面上的一个燃烧洞穴。

I admit, it has been rather disappointing that the general media has mostly ignored this issue. Weak, cautious "are we in an AI bubble?" conversation is insufficient for the level of collapse we're staring down.

我承认，大众媒体大多忽视了这个问题，这让我相当失望。软弱、谨慎的"我们是否在AI泡沫中？"的对话不足以应对我们面临的崩溃可能性。

Today, I will dive deep into the reality of AI costs, and explain in uncomfortably granular detail how easily these data centers can rapidly become insolvent should their tenants fail to pay.

今天，我将深入探讨AI成本的现实，并以令人不安的细节解释这些数据中心在租户未能付款时如何轻易地迅速走向资不抵债。

The [Chain of Pain](https://www.wheresyoured.at/the-enshittifinancial-crisis/#the-%E2%80%9Cchain-of-pain%E2%80%9D-that-bursts-the-ai-bubble) is real:

[痛苦链条](https://www.wheresyoured.at/the-enshittifinancial-crisis/#the-%E2%80%9Cchain-of-pain%E2%80%9D-that-bursts-the-ai-bubble)是真实存在的：

> These GPUs are predominantly purchased using debt provided by banks or financial institutions. While hyperscalers can and do use cash flow to purchase GPUs, even they are beginning to turn to debt. At that point, the company that buys the GPUs spends hundreds of millions of dollars building data centers, then, once operational, provides compute to model providers, who then sell access to those GPUs at a loss. For example, both OpenAI and Anthropic lose billions of dollars, and both rely on venture capital to fund their continued ability to pay for access to these GPUs.
>
> 这些GPU主要是使用银行或金融机构提供的债务购买的。虽然超大规模云计算公司可以而且确实使用现金流为GPU提供资金，但即使他们也开始转向债务。到那时，购买GPU的公司投入数亿美元建设数据中心，一旦启动，就向模型提供商提供计算服务，然后模型提供商开始亏损出售对这些GPU的访问权。例如，OpenAI和Anthropic都亏损数十亿美元，而且都依赖风险投资来资助他们继续支付访问这些GPU的能力。

> At that point, OpenAI and Anthropic provide subscriptions — which cost far more to provide than they make in revenue — or API access to their models on a per-million-token basis. AI startups pay to access these models to run their services, ultimately costing more than they make in revenue, meaning they must raise venture capital to continue paying for access to these models.
>
> 到那时，OpenAI和Anthropic提供订阅——其提供成本远高于收入——或按每百万token为基础提供对其模型的API访问。AI初创公司支付费用访问这些模型来运行他们的服务，最终成本高于他们获得的收入，这意味着他们必须筹集风险投资才能继续支付访问这些模型的费用。

> Outside of hyperscalers using cash flow to purchase GPUs from Nvidia, no part of the AI industry is revenue-driven. Every single part of the industry is driven by some kind of subsidy.
>
> 除了超大规模云计算公司使用现金流向英伟达购买GPU之外，AI行业的任何部分都不是由收入驱动的。该行业的每一个部分都是由某种补贴驱动的。

As such, the AI bubble is effectively a stress test on global venture capital, private equity, private credit, institutional and banking systems and their willingness to subsidize this forever, because not a single generative AI company has a path to profitability.

因此，AI泡沫实际上是对全球风险投资、私募股权、私人信贷、机构和银行系统及其永远资助这一切的意愿的压力测试，因为没有一家生成式AI公司有盈利路径。

Today I'll explain how easily it all comes crashing down.

今天我将解释它有多容易崩溃。

## 批判性思考评论 / Critical Thinking Commentary

### 作者的主要论点分析

本文作者Ed Zitron提出了一个关于AI行业财务可持续性的强烈警告。他的核心论点可以总结为以下几点：

1. **AI公司的商业模式不可持续**：OpenAI、Anthropic等头部AI实验室都在以惊人的速度烧钱，训练成本被故意排除在毛利率计算之外，造成财务假象。

2. **数据中心建设存在巨大金融风险**：以Oracle的Stargate项目为例，建设成本远超融资能力，债务负担沉重，利润率微薄。

3. **整个AI生态系统依赖外部补贴**：从GPU采购到数据中心运营，再到模型提供商和AI初创公司，整个行业链条都靠风险投资、债务和大型科技公司的补贴维持，而非真正的收入驱动。

4. **"痛苦链条"理论**：作者构建了一个层层传导的风险链条，一旦某个环节断裂，整个生态系统可能崩溃。

### 文章的优点

1. **数据详实，论证具体**：作者引用了大量具体数字——Anthropic的45亿美元收入与52亿美元亏损、2000亿美元的总支出预测、Oracle 27.2%的毛利率等，增强了说服力。

2. **财务分析专业**：对"训练成本"会计处理的批评非常尖锐。指出将训练成本排除在毛利率之外是一种"欺骗性"做法，这一点对于理解AI公司的真实财务状况至关重要。

3. **揭露了行业话术**：作者敏锐地捕捉到AI公司高管言论与实际行动之间的矛盾——一边宣称专注收入增长而非花钱，一边做出数百亿美元的资本承诺。

4. **模型构建有价值**：作者自行构建的AI数据中心成本模型，为理解这个复杂行业提供了量化工具。

### 文章的弱点与局限

1. **可能存在 Confirmation Bias（确认偏误）**：作者此前多次表达对AI的怀疑态度（文中链接可证），这可能导致他在分析中选择性地关注负面数据。对于AI可能带来的效率提升和新收入模式，文章着墨甚少。

2. **忽视了技术发展的非线性特征**：历史上许多 transformative technology（变革性技术）在早期都经历了巨额投入和亏损的阶段（如亚马逊、特斯拉）。作者假设当前的成本结构会永久持续，可能低估了技术进步和规模效应带来的成本下降潜力。

3. **对"成功"的定义过于狭隘**：文章将AI"成功"等同于当前的商业模式盈利，但大型科技公司可能将AI视为战略性投入，愿意长期补贴以获取未来竞争优势。

4. **债务分析可能过于悲观**：虽然债务水平令人担忧，但大型科技公司和数据中心的融资成本相对较低，且资产具有实物价值，与纯粹的金融泡沫有所不同。

### 我的批判视角

作为读者，我认为这篇文章是一剂必要的"冷水"，但可能过于悲观。以下是几点补充思考：

1. **关于训练成本的会计处理**：虽然作者的观点有一定道理，但将大规模预训练成本视为"原材料成本"也有争议。预训练模型的知识可以被多次复用，类似于软件开发的初始投入，而非每次推理都需要新的"原材料"。更合理的做法可能是将训练成本视为需要在模型生命周期内摊销的资本支出。

2. **关于Anthropic的公关策略**：作者对Dario Amodei言论的批评有一定道理，但将技术乐观主义等同于"不诚实"可能过于严苛。技术领导者对未来的预测往往具有倡导性质，而非纯粹的财务预测。

3. **关于数据中心的"燃烧洞穴"比喻**：这个比喻虽然生动，但忽视了数据中心的物理资产价值。即使AI需求不及预期，这些设施也可以 repurposed（改作他用）用于其他计算密集型任务。

4. **关于时间的考验**：作者的核心预测——AI行业将在债务压力下崩溃——是一个可验证的预测。如果到2028-2030年，主要AI公司仍未找到盈利路径且融资枯竭，那么他的分析将被证明是有先见之明的。反之，如果行业通过技术突破或商业模式创新实现了可持续盈利，那么这篇文章将成为技术怀疑论的典型案例。

### 深层启示

无论预测是否准确，这篇文章提出了一个重要的系统性风险问题：当整个行业的价值链条都依赖外部融资而非内部现金流时，这个行业确实脆弱。这不仅是AI行业的问题，也是现代金融体系"繁荣-萧条"周期的缩影。

对于投资者、政策制定者和从业者而言，文章的价值在于提醒我们需要问自己：如果风险投资明天枯竭，这个行业还剩下什么？这个问题的答案，将决定AI究竟是下一个互联网革命，还是又一个被过度炒作的泡沫。
